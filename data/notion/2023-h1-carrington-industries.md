# Carrington Industries — Engagement Notes (2023 H1)

**Client:** Carrington Industries — specialty fasteners manufacturer, Cleveland OH
**Engagement type:** Lean inventory + S&OP redesign
**Duration:** 12 weeks (Jan 16 – Apr 7, 2023)
**Fee:** $135k
**Outcome:** $2.4M annualized inventory carrying-cost reduction; S&OP cadence rebuilt

---

## Final findings

The headline finding was **WIP inventory excess driven by misaligned S&OP cadence**. Carrington was running a "monthly demand review + weekly supply review" pattern that was creating a 6-week feedback delay between demand signals and procurement actions. The bullwhip effect on raw-material orders was real and quantified: finished-goods variance was ±8% week-over-week, but raw-steel orders were swinging ±35%.

**Three recommendations, all adopted:**

1. **Collapse to weekly integrated S&OP.** Single 90-minute Tuesday meeting with sales, ops, procurement, finance. Demand-supply-capacity all in one room. Killed the lag.

2. **Rebuild the demand signal at the SKU-family level.** Carrington had been forecasting at the customer level (which is too noisy for a 4-week lead time) AND at the total-product level (too coarse to drive raw-material decisions). Family-level was the right granularity. Implemented in NetSuite via custom forecast roll-ups.

3. **Buffer-inventory rationalization.** Cut WIP by 28% by setting role-specific reorder points — fast-movers held at 2-week buffer, slow-movers at 6-week. Slow-mover SKUs cut even further once we identified ~60 dead-stock SKUs that hadn't shipped in 18 months.

## What didn't go well

The CFO (Lina Voss) pushed hard for ABC analysis on raw materials in Week 4 — wanted to expand scope to procurement supplier consolidation. I pushed back and kept us focused on inventory + S&OP because the bullwhip was the bigger lever. She accepted but it was tense for 3 weeks. **Lesson: when the client's CFO wants a scope expansion mid-engagement, deflect to a follow-on engagement framing rather than fighting the request directly.**

## Quantified result

- Inventory carrying cost reduced from $9.8M annual → $7.4M annual
- WIP turns improved from 8.2x → 11.5x
- Forecast error (MAPE) at SKU-family level: 14% (vs 22% baseline)
- ROI on engagement: 17.7x ($2.4M annualized savings / $135k engagement fee)
