# MidStates Plastics — Engagement Notes (2023 Q4)

**Client:** MidStates Plastics — injection molding for automotive + appliance OEMs, St Louis MO
**Engagement type:** Procurement optimization + supplier consolidation
**Duration:** 10 weeks (Oct 9 – Dec 15, 2023)
**Fee:** $122k
**Outcome:** $1.8M annualized raw-material cost reduction; supplier base consolidated from 47 to 18 active suppliers

---

## The supplier consolidation recommendations

MidStates had inherited a fragmented supplier base from a 2018 acquisition (acquired Pinnacle Molding, never fully integrated). Their resin procurement spread across **47 active suppliers** for what should have been roughly 12-15 SKU families. Per-supplier order volumes were below leverage thresholds; they were paying ~12% above market for HDPE, polypropylene, and ABS.

After 4 weeks of spend analysis (which lives in `Drive/MidStates Spend Cube Q3 2023.xlsx`), I made three recommendations:

1. **Consolidate to 12-18 strategic suppliers across all categories.** Tier 1 (8 suppliers, 80% of spend) — relationship-managed, quarterly reviews, multi-year terms. Tier 2 (8-10 suppliers, 18% of spend) — annual rate cards, transactional. Tier 3 (≤2 suppliers, 2% of spend) — specialty / one-off, ad hoc.

2. **Run an RFP for the top 3 commodity categories** — HDPE, PP, ABS. These three resin families were 65% of MidStates' raw-material spend. RFP results landed market rates 9-14% better than incumbent prices.

3. **Implement a 90-day supplier-performance scorecard** — on-time delivery, defect rate, price-vs-index. Trigger for tier demotion or rotation. Owned by procurement (Sergei Kovalenko).

All three adopted. The RFP closed in late November 2023; new contracts effective January 2024. Sergei ran the scorecard from go-live.

## Quantified result

- Raw-material spend reduced from $14.6M → $12.8M annual run rate
- Tier 1 suppliers earned a small premium over Tier 2 in exchange for guaranteed-quarterly-volume contracts (net: better prices than the spot market on tier 1)
- ROI on engagement: 14.8x ($1.8M annualized savings / $122k engagement fee)
- Supplier count reduced from 47 → 18 active (38% reduction in supplier admin overhead)

## What didn't go well

The owner (Pete DiMarco, second-generation) wanted to keep three local relationships that didn't make Tier 1 on the data. They were 25-year supplier relationships, family friendships. I pushed back on two of them and conceded on the third (a small specialty-resin supplier that had legitimately unique formulations for MidStates' premium appliance customers). **Lesson: in family-owned businesses, the long-relationship suppliers carry political weight that doesn't show up in the spend data. Don't try to fight all of them at once. Pick the ones with the most economic damage and concede the rest.**

## Stakeholders

- **Pete DiMarco** (Owner / President) — sponsor; second-generation owner, very people-oriented, sees suppliers as relationships not vendors. See "What didn't go well."
- **Sergei Kovalenko** (Director of Procurement) — engagement co-lead; sharp, results-driven, slightly impatient with the "but we've worked with them for 20 years" conversations. Owned the scorecard rollout flawlessly.
- **Marie Bouchard** (Plant Manager, St Louis) — supportive; worried about supply continuity during the transition; legitimate concern, addressed via a 60-day overlap period for tier 1 changes.
