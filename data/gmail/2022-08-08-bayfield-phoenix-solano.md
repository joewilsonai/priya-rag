Sara — this is the right question and I want to answer it honestly.

You're not being precious. The brand-story argument is real. I've watched two craft brewery clients lose distribution-control narrative and watch the brand soften over the next 24 months. It's quiet but measurable.

That said: I'd argue the brand story doesn't have to be "we control distribution everywhere." The stronger story might be "we control distribution where we can do it better than anyone — LA and SF." The Phoenix outsourcing then becomes a feature, not a footnote: Bayfield is selective about where it goes direct because it cares about the experience, not the geography.

On the data: Phoenix accounts have already been the biggest churn driver for you (4 lost accounts in 12 months, all citing reliability). Keeping the route in-house and breaking 3-day promises 40% of the time is also a brand-story problem, just a quieter one. Reliability is part of the story too.

If you want to go further on protecting the experience: build a bi-monthly Phoenix tasting tour for retail accounts, ride along with Solano twice a year, let Phoenix retailers see Bayfield humans in their stores even when the truck doesn't say Bayfield on the side. That's the version that lets you keep the story while letting Solano do the truck work.

Diego and I aligned on this Tuesday — if you greenlight, we cut over to Solano for Phoenix on Sept 1. Decision needs to be made this week.
