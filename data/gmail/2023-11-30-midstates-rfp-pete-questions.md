All fair questions. Worth pushing on. Here's where I am:

Recommend keeping them as a Tier 2 supplier on a 12-month rate-card with minimum quarterly volumes. They lose the strategic-Tier-1 status (no multi-year guarantee, no quarterly review meetings) but they keep enough volume to remain healthy. Net incremental cost vs full cut: ~$45k/year — small enough to absorb in the brand-equity / relationship line.

My recommendation: have a private conversation with their president (Bob Kessel) before we send the formal Tier-3 notice. Tell him directly what we're seeing. Two outcomes: either he matches the market and stays Tier 2, or he loses the volume gracefully and gets 6 months to find replacement customers. Either way, the relationship survives.
